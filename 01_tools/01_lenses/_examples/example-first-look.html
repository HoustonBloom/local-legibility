Growth guide · know what to add, and when

Make the system work better as you grow.

Nothing here is required today. Each item tells you why it matters and at what scale. Do them as you hit the thresholds, not before.

1

Add a visibility: field to files you'd be happy to share publicly.

Nice to test · Required to publish

Why it matters: The visibility array is how the packet knows what to include in a cut and what the preview site is allowed to publish. Without it, the cuts skill can still read filenames and headings : but a tag makes inclusion deliberate.

When it matters: Not yet. You're still exploring. The moment you want an external-facing frame or you spin up the local preview site, this becomes required on every file that goes public.

Add to the top of each file
--- visibility: [local, public] ---
2

Add a short intake.md beside each PDF or non-markdown file summarizing its key points.

At scale · Needed at 10+ non-markdown files
You: 2 non-markdown files Threshold: 10
20% of threshold. Plenty of runway. Revisit when you pass ~8.

Why it matters: Cuts work best with markdown text. A 200-word summary gives lenses strong material to work from : and keeps the originals untouched. Summaries let the skill pick the right files to include at full depth.

When it matters: Right now the packet can read your 2 PDFs inline : no bottleneck. Once you pass ~10, reading every file during every cut starts burning context unnecessarily. Summaries become the efficient way in.

Top of each intake.md
--- visibility: [local, public] type: summary source: ../path/to/original.pdf ---
3

Create a Daily/Changelog.md with 2-3 lines per day.

Nice to have · Unlocks timeline view

Why it matters: This powers the Activity Timeline lens and the timeline widget in the local preview site. It also helps the cuts skill know which projects are active versus dormant.

When it matters: Optional while you're in a single folder. Becomes high-value once you have 3+ projects running in parallel : the changelog is the only cross-project pulse the packet can read at a glance.
