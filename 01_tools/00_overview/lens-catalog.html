Active Lens · Company Website

Company Website

A multi-page marketing site for a company with multiple offerings. Bundled as one shareable HTML file.

What is it

5 pages typical: Home, 2-3 Offerings (under a dropdown), an optional flagship deep-dive, and an About / Vision page. Single-row sticky nav with mobile hamburger.

Why use it

When you have multiple product lines or services and want a structured exploration. Each offering gets its own page with depth. Send the whole site as one file.

Ready Lens · Pitch

Pitch

Story-driven slideshow for live pitch delivery. 11 slides, presenter mode, five capital-type variants.

What is it

11 slides across five movements: Title, Shift, Stakes, Promised Land, Why Now, Experience, Magic Gifts, Evidence, Peak, Ask, Close. Keyboard and swipe nav. Presenter mode (P · speaker notes, T · timer, F · fullscreen). Mobile-first.

Why use it

When you need to persuade live. Pitch competitions, investor meetings, partner buy-in, foundation pitches. Five capital-type variants (vc, impact, grant, crowdfund, steward) adjust emphasis and ask shape without changing the narrative spine.

Ready Lens · Reel

Reel

A full-viewport set of 5 to 9 scrollable slides with persistent bottom-bar navigation. Audience-facing, social-shareable.

What is it

Each slide stands on its own with a title, a tight body, and an optional visual. Swipe / arrow / keyboard navigation. Mobile-first.

Why use it

When you want to publish a story your audience can scroll through on a phone: an essay drop, a launch, a digest of curated links.

Ready Lens · Share

Share

Stumble through a capture. Markdown inline. HTML iframed. PDFs iframed or held as threads. Closes with a synthesis recap that weaves what matters across the set.

What is it

A preview-forward, one-item-at-a-time frame. The opener is either a single-statement bluff or a category chip row. Each content slide renders the item in one of three modes — inline, iframe, or summary + "holding the thread" — auto-picked by content type, with fallback when an iframe is blocked. The recap slide weaves threads and primes for next time.

Why use it

When the value of the share is the reader seeing the actual material, not a description of it. Session closeouts, collaborator handoffs, a batch of proposals threaded into one shareable URL. Distinct from Reel · Digest Mode, which is for curated external links.

Ready Lens · Reddit

Reddit

Vault content rendered as an authentic-feeling Reddit home feed. Claims in posts and comments are hyperlinked to their source markdown.

What is it

A feed of post cards using Reddit chrome (orange snoo, grey background, real subreddit icons). Each card opens to an individual post page with threaded comments between your configured personas. Butter-highlighted claims open the source markdown in a modal, scrolled to the section.

Why use it

When your reader engages better with discussion-forum chrome than essay chrome. The medium is the ask: unfamiliar-vault content surfaces in a form your audience already scrolls. First tested with [a named contact] — passed as real Reddit on sight. Use when a specific contact prefers this lens (check their Preferred Lens slot).

Ready Lens · Explainer

Explainer / Walk-through

Numbered "beats" walking through a concept, with file previews and hand-written margin notes.

What is it

Each beat shows the actual artifact (a file, a code block, a diagram), then annotates what matters. Teaches by demonstration.

Why use it

When you need to explain how something works to someone who'll learn faster from seeing the real thing: onboarding docs, peer guides.

Coming soon Lens · Observatory

Observatory

A live-status, multi-pane dashboard view of a single project or initiative.

What is it

Current state pane, open questions pane, recent activity timeline, and connections. Denser than a Recap, lighter than a full project hub.

Why use it

For a "where things are right now" snapshot. Good for stakeholder updates, weekly check-ins, or self-orientation when you return to a project after time away.

Coming soon Lens · Storyboard Editorial

Storyboard Editorial

Editorial storyboard frames for narrative use cases. Each frame carries one scene, one narrative, and one set of design principles.

What is it

Warm-cream editorial aesthetic with hand-crafted SVG illustration. Per frame: eyebrow label, serif italic title + dek, full-width scene SVG, sans-serif caption with time and place, prose body, principle chip row, "how the data moves" flow strip. Four bracket corners mark the frame's edges.

Why use it

When you need to walk a reader through a scene before explaining the system behind it. Pitches, presentations, essays where illustration carries emotional register rather than decorating. Do not use for technical documentation or data-heavy content.

Coming soon Lens · Activity Timeline

Activity Timeline

A vertical timeline of dated entries across a body of work. One axis, dot-coded by type, with short descriptions per entry.

What is it

Reads a dated log source (e.g., 02_activity/, Daily/Changelog.md) and renders the entries as a scrollable vertical timeline. Entries get color-coded dots by type (created, edited, decision, question, research). Good for reviews, audits, and "what happened this month" frames.

Why use it

When the interesting information is the shape of activity over time. Retrospectives, handoffs, or catching up a collaborator on a project's arc. Do not use for single-moment snapshots — use Observatory for that.
